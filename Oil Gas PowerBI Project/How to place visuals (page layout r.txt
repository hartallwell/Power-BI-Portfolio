How to place visuals (page layout rules)
Use this simple grid approach:

Top = summary, middle = explanations, bottom = details

Top row: KPI cards.
Middle row: 2–3 main charts showing trends and top breakdowns.
Bottom row: 2 more detailed visuals (matrix, scatter, detailed bar charts).
Left to right = story

Left: “What and how big?” (incidents, cost, downtime).
Middle: “Where and on what?” (by facility, equipment).
Right: “Why / risk view?” (risk categories, high‑cost/high‑downtime items).
Slicers placement

Put key slicers (Date, Facility, Equipment, maybe Risk Category)
horizontally at the top or vertically in a narrow left pane.
Avoid scattering slicers across the page.
Keep it clean

Max ~6–8 visuals per page.
Align visuals in a grid, consistent heights and widths.
Leave white space; don’t edge visuals all the way to the border.
